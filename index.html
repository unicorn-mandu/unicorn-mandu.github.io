(Tentative) Modular architecture for non-prehensile manipulation

Yoonyoung Cho, Junhyek Han, Jisu Han, Beomjoon Kim,
KAIST
Paper arXiv Video Code Data

Abstract

Non-prehensile manipulation actions such as pushing and toppling are necessary when manipulating ungraspable objects in general environments like households. Despite this potential, prior works on non-prehensile manipulation cannot yet generalize across environments with diverse geometries. The primary challenge here is adapting to the varying environmental constraints: within a cabinet, the robot must avoid walls and ceilings; while lifting objects across steps, the robot must account for the bump's pose and extent. Accounting for many such constraint combinations that vary by each scene presents a learning challenge for the generalist policy, as it must learn many different strategies to address distinct sets of constraint combinations. To overcome this, we develop a deep reinforcement learning framework based on a modular hypernetwork architecture, which reconfigures the policy according to the current task requirements. Further, we propose a contact-based representation for environment geometry, learned a priori for practical point-cloud-based policy training. To train our policy, we additionally propose a procedural generation scheme for diverse environmental geometry. Our real-world results show that the resulting policy can transfer zero-shot to novel real-world environments.
